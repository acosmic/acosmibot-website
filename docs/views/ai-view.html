AI Integration

Connect Acosmibot to an AI model and let your members have natural conversations directly in Discord — mention the bot anywhere and it responds with context-aware replies shaped by your custom instructions.

Overview

Acosmibot's AI integration gives your server a conversational AI assistant that lives right inside Discord. Members interact with it naturally by mentioning the bot, and admins have full control over which model it uses, how it behaves, where it responds, and how much it's used each day.

Key Features

  • Responds to any message that mentions the bot
  • Maintains context for the last 15 messages per channel
  • Conversation history auto-clears after 6 hours of inactivity
  • Built-in 3-second per-user cooldown to prevent spam
  • Configurable daily usage limit for the entire server
  • Custom system prompt to define the AI's personality and rules
  • Restrict AI responses to specific channels
  • Swap the underlying AI model at any time

How It Works

1

Enable AI for Your Server

Use /ai-enable to turn on AI responses. Once enabled, the bot will begin watching for mentions across all channels (or only the channels you restrict it to).

2

Members Mention the Bot

Any member can start a conversation simply by mentioning the bot in a message — no slash command required. The bot reads the recent message history in that channel to maintain context across a conversation.

3

Context is Maintained Automatically

The bot tracks the last 15 messages in each channel as conversation context. If a channel goes quiet for more than 6 hours, the history is cleared automatically so the next conversation starts fresh. Members can also clear their own session with /reset-ai-thread.

4

Rate Limits Keep Things Running Smoothly

A built-in 3-second cooldown per user prevents rapid-fire spam. Admins can also set a server-wide daily interaction cap with /ai-limit to keep API costs predictable.

Commands

/ai-enable
Enable AI responses for the server. Requires administrator permissions. Once enabled, the bot will respond to mentions in all permitted channels.

Example:

/ai-enable
/ai-disable
Disable AI responses for the server. The bot will stop responding to mentions until re-enabled. Existing configuration settings are preserved.

Example:

/ai-disable
/ai-status
Check whether AI is currently enabled and view a summary of your current settings — active model, daily limit, usage today, and any channel restrictions in place.

Example:

/ai-status
/ai-limit <number>
Set the maximum number of AI interactions allowed across the entire server per day. Once the limit is reached, the bot will stop responding until the counter resets at midnight. Set to 0 to remove the limit.

Examples:

/ai-limit 100 /ai-limit 0
/ai-model <model>
Change which AI model the bot uses for responses. Different models vary in capability, speed, and cost. Check /ai-status to see the currently active model.

Example:

/ai-model gpt-4o-mini
/ai-instructions <prompt>
Set a custom system prompt that shapes how the AI behaves — its personality, tone, areas of focus, things it should never do, and any server-specific context. This is how you make the AI feel like it belongs to your community.

Example:

/ai-instructions You are Cosmo, a friendly assistant for the Acosmibot gaming community. Keep answers concise and always stay on topic.
/ai-clear
Clear the conversation history for the current channel. Useful when a conversation has gone off the rails or you want the AI to start fresh without waiting for the 6-hour auto-clear.

Example:

/ai-clear
/reset-ai-thread
Reset your personal AI session. Any member can use this to clear their own conversation context without affecting others in the channel.

Example:

/reset-ai-thread

Channel Restrictions

By default, the bot can respond to mentions in any channel. You can restrict AI responses to specific channels so the bot only engages where you want it to — for example, a dedicated #ask-ai channel.

Setting Up Channel Restrictions

  • Channel restrictions are configured through the /server dashboard
  • Add one or more allowed channels — the bot will ignore mentions outside of them
  • To remove restrictions and allow all channels, clear the allowed-channels list
  • The bot will silently ignore mentions in non-permitted channels (no error message)

Writing a Good System Prompt

The system prompt set with /ai-instructions is the most powerful tool for customizing your AI's behavior. Here are some examples of what you can define:

You are a helpful assistant for [Server Name]. You only answer questions related to [your topic]. Keep responses under 3 sentences. Always be friendly and encouraging. Never discuss competitor products.

Tips for Effective Instructions

  • Give the AI a name and personality that fits your community's vibe
  • Specify the scope of topics the AI should focus on or avoid
  • Set a tone — casual, professional, humorous, concise, etc.
  • Include any server-specific context the AI should know (game name, rules, lore, etc.)
  • Define what the AI should do when asked something out of scope

Troubleshooting

Q: The bot isn't responding when I mention it

A: Check that AI is enabled with /ai-status. If channel restrictions are configured, confirm you're mentioning the bot in an allowed channel. Also verify the daily usage limit hasn't been reached.

Q: The AI seems to have forgotten our earlier conversation

A: Context is limited to the last 15 messages per channel and clears automatically after 6 hours of inactivity. If the conversation was long or old, the earlier context is no longer available.

Q: How do I stop the AI from responding in a specific channel without restricting all channels?

A: Set up a channel allow-list in the dashboard that includes only the channels where you want the bot to respond. Any channel not on the list will be ignored.

Q: The bot says the daily limit has been reached

A: The server-wide interaction counter resets at midnight. You can raise or remove the limit with /ai-limit if the current cap is too low for your server's activity.

Q: Can members change the AI model or system prompt?

A: No. The /ai-model and /ai-instructions commands require administrator permissions. Only admins can change the model or system prompt.
