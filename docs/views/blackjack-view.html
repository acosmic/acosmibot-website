Blackjack

Play a full-featured game of Blackjack against the dealer using standard casino rules — hit, stand, double down, split pairs, take insurance, or surrender, all with your server credits on the line.

Overview

Acosmibot's Blackjack game brings a complete casino-style experience to your Discord server. Members wager server credits against a virtual dealer in a full implementation of the game, including advanced options like splitting pairs, doubling down, insurance, and surrender. Games are played interactively using Discord's button interface — no extra commands needed mid-hand.

How to Play

1

Place Your Bet

Start a game with /blackjack <bet>, wagering between 50 and 10,000 credits. The credits are immediately deducted from your balance. The dealer and player are each dealt two cards — the dealer's second card is face down (the hole card).

2

Choose Your Action

Use the interactive buttons that appear below the game embed to take your turn. Depending on your hand and the situation, you may have access to Hit, Stand, Double Down, Split, Insurance, and Surrender. Options that aren't available for your current hand are automatically hidden.

3

Dealer Plays Out

Once you stand (or bust), the dealer flips their hole card and plays out their hand according to fixed rules: they must hit on 16 or lower and stand on 17 or higher. The dealer does not make strategic choices — their play is fully deterministic.

4

Result and Payout

The hand closest to 21 without going over wins. Winnings are credited to your balance immediately. A push (tie) returns your original bet. A natural Blackjack on your first two cards pays 1.5× your wager.

Advanced Actions Explained

Double Down
Available on your first two cards only. Doubles your original bet — you receive exactly one more card and then automatically stand. Best used when you have a strong starting total (9, 10, or 11) and the dealer is showing a weak card.

Example:

Bet: 200 → Double Down → New bet: 400 → One card dealt → Stand
Split
Available when your first two cards are a matching pair. Splits your hand into two independent hands, each with its own bet equal to your original wager. Each hand is then played out separately against the dealer.

Example:

Pair of 8s + bet 300 → Split → Two hands of 8, each with 300 bet
Insurance
Offered when the dealer's face-up card is an Ace. You can place a side bet worth up to half your original wager. If the dealer has Blackjack, insurance pays 2:1 (covering your main bet loss). If the dealer does not have Blackjack, the insurance bet is lost and the main hand continues.

Example:

Bet: 200 → Insurance: 100 → Dealer has BJ → Insurance pays 200 → Net: even
Surrender
Available on your first two cards only. Forfeit the hand immediately and recover half of your original bet. Useful when you have a bad starting hand against a strong dealer card and want to cut your losses.

Example:

Bet: 400 → Surrender → Lose 200, recover 200

Strategy Tips

Basic Strategy Pointers

  • Always stand on 17 or higher — the risk of busting outweighs the potential gain
  • Double down on 11 whenever possible — statistically one of the strongest moves
  • Split Aces and 8s — Aces give you two strong starts; 8s escape a weak 16
  • Never split 10s — a 20 is already an excellent hand
  • Use Surrender on 16 vs. dealer 9, 10, or Ace — cutting losses is the right play here
  • Avoid Insurance unless you're counting cards — statistically a poor bet over time

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't chase losses by betting the maximum on every hand after a losing streak
  • Avoid hitting on 12–16 when the dealer shows a 4, 5, or 6 — let the dealer bust
  • Don't assume the dealer's hole card is always a 10 — play the probabilities, not assumptions
  • Never go all-in on a single hand if your credit balance is tight

Troubleshooting

Q: The game buttons disappeared before I could take my turn

A: Game sessions time out after a period of inactivity to free up resources. If the buttons are gone, the hand has been forfeited. Start a new game with /blackjack.

Q: I don't have enough credits to play

A: The minimum bet is 50 credits. Earn more credits through the server economy — check commands like /daily or other economy features your server has enabled.

Q: The Split option isn't showing up even though I have a pair

A: Split requires enough credits to place a second bet equal to your original wager. If your balance is lower than your bet amount, the Split option will not be available.

Q: What happens to my credits if the bot goes offline mid-game?

A: If a game is interrupted by downtime, the bet may be held until the session clears. Contact server support or wait for the session to expire — credits are not permanently lost.

Q: Can multiple people play Blackjack at the same time?

A: Yes — each /blackjack command starts an independent game for that member. Multiple players can have active games simultaneously without interfering with each other.
