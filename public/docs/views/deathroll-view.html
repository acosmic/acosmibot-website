Deathroll

A head-to-head betting duel where two players take turns rolling a shrinking die. One wrong roll ends it all.

Overview

Deathroll is Acosmibot's most intense gambling game — a tense, back-and-forth duel between two players. The stakes are high, the rolls keep shrinking, and one unlucky 1 ends it all. Challenge a friend, accept the risk, and may the odds be with you.

Key Features

  • Head-to-head PvP betting — challenge any server member
  • Shrinking roll range creates mounting tension each turn
  • Interactive button prompt for the challenged player to accept
  • 120-second acceptance window before the challenge expires
  • Minimum bet of 100 credits
  • Loser pays the winner the full bet amount

How to Play

1

Issue the Challenge

Use /deathroll to challenge another member and specify the bet amount. The minimum wager is 100 credits. Both players must have enough credits to cover the bet.

/deathroll @username 500
2

Accept the Challenge

The challenged player receives a message with an Accept button. They have 120 seconds to accept. If they don't respond in time, the challenge expires and no credits are exchanged.

3

Take Turns Rolling

The game begins with a roll range of 1 to the bet amount. The challenger rolls first. Whatever number they roll becomes the new maximum for the next roll. Players alternate turns.

4

Survive the 1

The first player to roll a 1 loses the game and pays the full bet to their opponent. As the range shrinks, the chance of rolling a 1 increases — the final rolls are the most nerve-wracking.

Commands

/deathroll <user> <bet>
Challenge another member to a Deathroll duel for the specified credit amount. Both players must have sufficient credits. Minimum bet is 100 credits.

Examples:

/deathroll @Clint 100 /deathroll @username 5000

Game Rules

Rule Detail
Starting Range 1 to the bet amount (e.g., bet 500 → first roll is 1–500)
Roll Shrink Each roll result becomes the new maximum for the next player's roll
Losing Condition Rolling a 1 — that player loses the full bet
Minimum Bet 100 credits
Acceptance Window 120 seconds — challenge expires if not accepted
Turn Order The challenger rolls first

Example Round

🎲 Sample Game (500 credit bet)

  • Player A rolls 1–500 → lands on 312
  • Player B rolls 1–312 → lands on 87
  • Player A rolls 1–87 → lands on 14
  • Player B rolls 1–14 → lands on 3
  • Player A rolls 1–3 → lands on 1 💀 — Player A loses!
  • Player B wins 500 credits.

Tips & Strategy

💡 Playing Smart

  • Higher bets increase the starting range, giving more room before the pressure builds
  • There's no guaranteed strategy — luck dominates once the range gets small
  • Only challenge players who have enough credits to cover the bet
  • Watch the range carefully — when it drops below 10, every roll matters enormously
  • Accept challenges quickly — you only have 120 seconds before it expires

⚠️ Credit Requirements

Both you and your opponent must have enough credits in your wallet (not the bank) to cover the full bet before the game can start. Withdraw from the bank if needed using /withdraw.

Troubleshooting

Q: My challenge expired — what happened to my credits?

A: No credits are exchanged when a challenge expires. The 120-second window passed without your opponent accepting, so everything stays as-is.

Q: Can I challenge a bot or myself?

A: No. Deathroll requires two real, distinct server members. You cannot challenge yourself or bots.

Q: My opponent doesn't have enough credits — can we still play?

A: No. Both players must have the full bet amount available in their wallet. The bot will inform you if either player's balance is insufficient.

Q: Can I cancel a challenge after sending it?

A: Challenges expire automatically after 120 seconds if not accepted. There is no manual cancel button — just wait it out.
