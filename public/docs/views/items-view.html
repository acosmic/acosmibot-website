Items & Inventory

A credit-funded shop where members buy items, keep them in a personal inventory, and activate effects that boost the XP and economy systems.

Overview

The items system lets members spend their credits on items that do something — most notably XP and credit boosts. Items live in a per-member inventory and are activated in one of two ways: used (a one-time consumable effect) or equipped (an effect that stays active while the item is equipped). Items can also be gifted or traded to other members.

Key Features

  • Browse and buy items with credits via /shop and /buy
  • Personal inventory per member with /inventory
  • Consumable items with one-time effects via /use
  • Equippable items with always-on effects via /equip and /unequip
  • Effects boost XP and credit earnings, with global or server scope
  • Gift items to anyone with /gift, or trade tradeable items with /trade

How It Works

🛍️ Buying items

Run /shop to see what's available and how much each item costs in credits. Buy one with /buy <item> — the cost is deducted from your server wallet and the item is added to your inventory. Each item has a rarity (common, rare, and so on) and a maximum stack size.

⚡ Using vs. equipping

How an item activates depends on its type:

  • Consumables — /use spends one copy and applies its effect immediately (for example, a temporary XP boost for a set duration).
  • Equippable — /equip turns the item's effect on and keeps it active until you /unequip it. Equipped items aren't consumed.

📈 Effects & boosts

An item's power lives in its effects — things like an xp_boost or credit_boost with a magnitude, a scope, and (for consumables) a duration. Boosts are additive and stack with the leveling and economy systems. Scope determines where a boost applies:

  • Server scope — the boost only applies in the server where it was activated.
  • Global scope — the boost follows you across every server running Acosmibot.

Commands

/shop
Browse the items you can buy with credits, including each item's price, rarity, and what it does.

Example:

/shop
/buy <item>
Buy an item from the shop with credits. The cost is deducted from your server wallet and the item is added to your inventory.

Example:

/buy XP Booster
/inventory
View the items you currently own, including quantities and which items are equipped.

Example:

/inventory
/use <item>
Use a consumable item to activate its effect. One copy is spent and the effect applies immediately (for example, a timed XP boost).

Example:

/use XP Booster
/equip <item>
Equip an item — its effect stays active while equipped. Equipped items are not consumed.

Example:

/equip Lucky Charm
/unequip <item>
Unequip an item — its effect turns off and the item returns to your inventory.

Example:

/unequip Lucky Charm
/gift <member> <item>
Give one of your giftable items to another member at no cost to them.

Example:

/gift @username XP Booster
/trade <member> <item>
Trade a tradeable item to another member of this server.

Example:

/trade @username Lucky Charm

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between using and equipping an item?

A: Using a consumable spends one copy and applies its effect right away (often for a limited time). Equipping keeps an item's effect active without consuming it — until you unequip it.

Q: Do boosts stack?

A: Yes. Boost magnitudes are additive and stack with the XP and economy systems, as well as with other active boosts.

Q: Can I move items between servers?

A: Trades happen between members of the same server. A boost's reach depends on its scope — server-scoped boosts only apply where they're activated, while global-scoped boosts follow you everywhere.

Q: What's the difference between gifting and trading?

A: Both move an item to another member. Gifting works with items flagged as giftable; trading works with items flagged as tradeable. An item's flags determine which actions are allowed.
